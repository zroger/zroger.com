Phase2 Technology (formerly Treehouse Agency), New York, NY

Director of Engineering / 2010 - Present

As Director of Engineering at Phase2 (and Treehouse), I am responsible for ensuring that my teams deliver the highest quality work for our clients. I am involved in all phases of project delivery as well as the personal development of team members.

  • Energy.gov

    I was the chief architect and technical analyst for this project to build a modern CMS for the U.S. Department of Energy. The project involved the development of a custom Drupal-based CMS, migration of content and files off of the legacy platform, and consolidation of hundreds of web properties into a single platform. This consolidation onto a single platform represents millions of dollars saved in hosting and development costs.

  • TheStreet.com

    I led the architecture and development of a new CMS for this Wall Street publishing company. They required a modern CMS to power the dozens of free and subscription sites that are the core of their business. My team built the first of these sites while the internal development team was trained and got comfortable with the new system. TheStreet now completely handles the development of their sites after a successful hand-off.

  • Anonymous Consumer Products Group

    I led a multi-year re-platforming effort for a billion-dollar consumer products group. The main focus of this project was to reduce the time and effort required to rollout new sites and redesigns. Before this platform, each new site (which included all new brands, countries and languages) took about 6 months and required very technical personnel. With the new platform, only new brand sites require technical personnel. Localized country sites and new languages are now simply content exercises.

  • Phase2 Project Automation

    In an effort to increase build quality and efficiency of our projects, I developed a library of automation tools tailored to the types of projects that we do. The current suite of tools includes PHPMD, PHPCS, Behat, SimpleTest and the Coder Drupal module. These tools are all tied together using Rake customized for each project. Finally, we have a set of standard Jenkins jobs that runs each of these Rake tasks and tracks the quality metrics.

When I left in 2010, there were nearly 200 sites running on the platform, with around 3 sites being launched every week.

Some of the sites have over 20 localized environments in over a dozen different languages. A lot of the work done for this platform made its way into Drupal 7.
